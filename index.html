1. Big Dreams Need Solid Proof

Investors want proof your business works and can grow.

  • Show real data, not just dreams.
  • Back your vision with evidence.

2. Common Mistakes

  • Talking too much about vision, not execution.
  • Not explaining fit with local market or laws.
  • Using unrealistic growth numbers.

3. What a Good Pitch Should Do

  • Clearly explain what your business does.
  • Show the problem you solve and why you're better.
  • Back it up with numbers (users, growth rate).

4. Start Strong

Grab attention with a clear, short explanation of your product or service.

  • Use real-life examples or numbers.
  • Show that people want your product.

5. Share Important Numbers

  • Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) vs. Lifetime Value (LTV).
  • Time to recover customer acquisition costs.
  • Monthly income, profit margin, retention rate.

6. How You’ll Use the Investment

Be specific. For example:

  • 40% to improve app/platform.
  • 30% to hire a better team.
  • 20% for legal compliance.
  • 10% for marketing.

Avoid vague statements like “grow the business.”

7. Stand Out from Others

  • Show what makes you better or different.
  • Explain your distribution strategy.
  • Be honest about challenges (e.g., laws, licenses).

8. End with a Clear Ask

Tell investors:

  • How much money you want.
  • What you’ll do with it.
  • What they’ll get in return.

9. Final Tip for Bangladeshi Founders

  • Mix big vision with solid numbers and a plan.
  • Tell your story well, but back it with facts.
  • Be honest, focused, and ready to learn.
